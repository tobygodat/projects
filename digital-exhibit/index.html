Curatorial Statement

Our exhibit analyzes how rejection can operate as a medium and form of social correction. Across many societies and cultures, individuals are expected to conform to certain social norms regarding work, relationships, and adulthood. Deviations from these expectations leads to subtle forms of rejection, which can take form of questions, criticism, or social pressure which encourage them to return to what society considers to be “normal”.

These reactions arise subtly in everyday settings, rather than coming across like direct and intentional punishment and conditioning. To exemplify this idea, our exhibit is structured as a convenience store inspired by Convenience Store Woman. Each aisle represents a different category of social expectation, relating to things like productivity, relationships, and behavioral norms.

Just as products are arranged in a store to be sold and accessed for easy consumption, these expectations are presented as ordinary and desirable life choices. By moving through our 5 aisles, viewers can observe how rejection functions as a subtle mechanism tending towards social correction, encouraging conformity.
